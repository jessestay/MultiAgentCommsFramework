# Utah Family Lawyer Key Conversation: Legal Strategy Overview

## Conversation Date: February-March 2025

## Context
This conversation series established the comprehensive legal strategy for Jesse's custody, parent-time enforcement, and financial dispute case, focusing on addressing Rebecca's parent-time violations, objecting to her planned relocation to St. George, modifying child support based on current income, and strategically using evidence of abuse and manipulation.

## Key Points
- Document and address Rebecca's repeated violations of the custody agreement
- Develop strategy to prevent relocation to St. George in June 2025
- Seek child support modification based on current self-employment income
- Strategically use evidence of Rebecca's abuse and manipulation
- Implement timeline to delay final decisions until after school starts in August 2025
- Address passport restrictions that impact Jesse's ability to work

## Conversation Transcript

### Jesse
Rebecca has frequently denied or interfered with my parent-time. Some of the major violations include:
- Taking the kids out of school on my Monday without notifying me.
- Signing them up for activities without my consent.
- Enrolling them in school without my approval despite joint legal custody.
- Leaving our child home alone while sick, refusing to let me care for them.
- Taking Joseph out of state during my scheduled Thanksgiving (2020), which I believe is felony custodial interference.

### Utah Family Lawyer
These are serious violations, particularly the out-of-state Thanksgiving issue. Under Utah Code § 76-5-303, removing a child from the state in violation of a custody order **can constitute custodial interference**, which is a **third-degree felony** if done intentionally. We should:
1. **File a Motion to Enforce Parent-Time** to document these violations officially.
2. **Consider filing a criminal complaint** for the Thanksgiving interference incident.
3. **Compile text messages and other evidence** that demonstrate Rebecca's repeated pattern of withholding parent-time.
4. **Ensure all filings include requests for attorney fees** due to enforcement costs.

### Jesse
Rebecca is planning to move the kids to St. George in June 2025. Her husband has been working there for months. She's already told the kids about the move without discussing it with me.

### Utah Family Lawyer
This is a **major relocation case** and will require:
- **An Objection to Relocation**, emphasizing the disruption to the children's stability, schooling, and social environment.
- **A Motion for Preliminary Injunction** to prevent relocation before trial.
- **Highlighting the children's best interests**—their entire lives are in Salt Lake Valley.
- **Delaying any final court order until August 2025**, ensuring they start school in Salt Lake.
- **Leveraging financial pressure**—forcing Rebecca to pay for a Custody Evaluator if she wants to modify custody.

### Jesse
What's the ideal legal timeline?

### Utah Family Lawyer
1. **March 15:** File the Objection to Relocation.
2. **April:** Rebecca's response (within 14 days).
3. **May:** Hearing—goal is for the court to say, "Not enough information, kids stay here."
4. **August 2025:** No final ruling before then, so the kids stay in Salt Lake.
5. **If Rebecca wants to fight it, she has to file a costly Petition to Modify.**

### Jesse
My child support is still based on my past income ($120K–170K/year), but I'm currently self-employed, earning around $2K–3K/month. ORS has restricted my passport, and Rebecca has denied my renewal before.

### Utah Family Lawyer
The immediate legal actions we need:
- **Motion for Temporary Child Support Modification** – Request recalculation based on current income.
- **Arguing financial hardship & passport restriction** in filings – The court must consider that you need to work.
- **Contesting any imputed income assumptions** – The court should not use past earnings if they are no longer reflective of actual financial circumstances.
- **Investigating whether Rebecca's attorney's actions contributed to job loss** – If so, there may be legal remedies.

### Jesse
I have video evidence from our marriage showing Rebecca screaming at, kicking, and manipulating our children. My oldest child has also posted TikTok videos talking about her abuse.

### Utah Family Lawyer
This is significant evidence that:
1. **Can impact custody evaluations.**  
2. **Might be useful at trial,** but timing and framing are critical.
3. **Should be cross-referenced with text messages and psychological reports** for credibility.

### Jesse
Should we introduce it immediately?

### Utah Family Lawyer
Not yet. The best approach is:
- **Keeping it as a strategic reserve** in case Rebecca tries to portray herself as a superior parent.
- **Presenting it in response to any claims against your parenting.**
- **Ensuring expert testimony backs up the impact on the children.**
- **Using it selectively in trial or custody evaluations.**

### Jesse
I want to ensure the court doesn't finalize anything before August 2025. If we get the kids started in school here, it makes it harder for Rebecca to argue for the move.

### Utah Family Lawyer
The key strategy is:
- **File the Objection by March 15, forcing a May hearing.**
- **Argue that the court needs more information before ruling.**
- **Ensure the process takes long enough that the kids remain in Salt Lake for school.**
- **If necessary, force Rebecca to file a Petition to Modify and require a costly Custody Evaluator.**

### Jesse
What's the risk if we move too fast?

### Utah Family Lawyer
If we file **too early**, the court could expedite the case, leading to:
- **A mini-trial in August, rather than delaying into the school year.**
- **A premature final order that Rebecca could exploit.**
- **A rushed decision that doesn't account for the full impact of relocation.**
