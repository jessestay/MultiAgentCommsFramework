# Utah Family Lawyer Key Conversation: Custody Support Modifications

## Conversation Date: March 2025

## Context
This conversation series addressed the legal strategy for modifying Jesse's child support obligations to reflect his current self-employment income rather than his previous higher income. The discussions covered the appropriate legal mechanisms, potential challenges from Rebecca, documentation requirements, and the intersection with the pending relocation case.

## Key Points
- Determine appropriate legal mechanism for child support modification
- Address potential claims of voluntary underemployment
- Gather and organize financial documentation to support modification
- Understand interaction between child support modification and relocation case
- Identify specific forms and filing requirements
- Prepare for potential opposition and hearing

## Conversation Transcript

### Jesse
I need to modify my child support payments to reflect my actual income. The current amount is based on an old, much higher income, and it's unsustainable. What's the best legal approach?

### Utah Family Lawyer
Utah law provides two primary methods for modifying child support:  
1. **Motion to Adjust Child Support** – Used if at least **three years** have passed since the last order and if there is a **10% or greater difference** between the current amount and what the guidelines would require based on your present income.  
2. **Petition to Modify Child Support** – Used **if less than three years** have passed or if there has been a **substantial change** (e.g., a 30% or more change in income, change in custody, or significant medical expenses).  

Which situation applies to you?

### Jesse
It has been more than three years, and my income is far lower now than when the original support amount was calculated. I am self-employed and don't have wages that can be garnished. The old income calculation was based on a job I no longer have.

### Utah Family Lawyer
In that case, you qualify for a **Motion to Adjust Child Support** based on the three-year rule. Here's what to do next:  
1. **Gather proof of income** – Recent tax returns, business income statements, and any documents showing changes in earnings.  
2. **Determine the new guideline amount** – The Utah Child Support Calculator can estimate your revised obligation.  
3. **File the Motion to Adjust Child Support** – Attach financial documents and request a hearing.  
4. **Prepare for opposition** – The other party may contest the adjustment, so having clear financial records is key.

### Jesse
What if Rebecca argues that I am voluntarily underemployed?

### Utah Family Lawyer
That is a common counterargument. Under Utah law, if a parent is found to be **intentionally earning less to reduce child support**, the court may **impute income** based on past earnings or available job opportunities.  

To **counter this**, we must prove:  
- **Your job loss or income reduction was involuntary** (e.g., layoff, business downturn).  
- **You have made reasonable efforts to earn at your full capacity** (job applications, business development efforts).  
- **Your current income is legitimate based on market conditions and your field of work**.  

A strong **paper trail of job applications, business revenue reports, and expert testimony** (if needed) will help prevent imputation.

### Jesse
What specific forms do I need to file?

### Utah Family Lawyer
For a **Motion to Adjust Child Support**, you need:  
- **Motion to Adjust Child Support** (Utah Courts form)  
- **Financial Declaration** (includes income, expenses, debts)  
- **Child Support Worksheet** (calculated based on new income)  
- **Supporting documentation** (tax returns, business statements, contracts, etc.)  

Once filed, the court will schedule a **hearing** where both parties can present their case. If Rebecca opposes, she may file a **response** contesting the adjustment.

### Jesse
If she delays or doesn't respond, what happens?

### Utah Family Lawyer
If she does not respond within the deadline, you can request a **default ruling in your favor**. If she responds but does not provide **strong counter-evidence**, the court will likely adjust the support based on your documented income.

### Jesse
Rebecca is also trying to move the kids to St. George. Would that affect child support?

### Utah Family Lawyer
Yes. If the court **approves** the relocation, child support calculations may change based on:  
- **Transportation costs for parent-time** – The court may order a modification to account for increased travel expenses.  
- **Time-sharing adjustments** – If parent-time changes, child support may be recalculated to reflect the new custody arrangement.  

Your **best approach** is to:  
1. **Object to the relocation** – File a **Motion for Orders Regarding Relocation** before the March 15 deadline.  
2. **Argue against excessive travel burdens** – Show how the move would interfere with your ability to exercise parent-time.  
3. **If relocation is granted, seek a child support adjustment** – Request modifications based on increased costs and changes in custody time.

### Jesse
So, to summarize my next actions:  
1. **File the Motion to Adjust Child Support** with financial documentation.  
2. **Object to the relocation before March 15** to prevent custody complications.  
3. **Prepare counterarguments if Rebecca claims I am underemployed.**  
4. **Monitor her response and push for a default ruling if she delays.**

### Utah Family Lawyer
Exactly. You should also:  
- **Keep records of income and business efforts** to defend against income imputation.  
- **Prepare for a court hearing** where both child support and relocation will be contested.  
- **Request that child support reflects travel costs** if relocation is allowed.  

Would you like assistance in drafting the filings?

## Decisions Made
- File Motion to Adjust Child Support based on three-year rule and significant income change
- Gather comprehensive financial documentation to support the motion
- Prepare defense against potential claims of voluntary underemployment
- Coordinate child support modification with objection to relocation
- Request consideration of transportation costs if relocation is approved
- Monitor for response deadlines and pursue default if appropriate
