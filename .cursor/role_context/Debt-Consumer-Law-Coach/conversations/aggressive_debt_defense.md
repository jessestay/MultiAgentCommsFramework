# Debt & Consumer Law Coach Key Conversation: Aggressive Debt Defense

## Conversation Date: February-March 2025

## Context
This conversation established the framework for an aggressive debt defense strategy, focusing on challenging creditor standing, using arbitration as a tactical weapon, exploiting procedural defenses, pursuing FDCPA and FCRA counterclaims, and preventing default judgments. The approach prioritizes saving money and turning the tables on debt collectors rather than passive acceptance or quick settlements.

## Key Points
- Challenge creditor standing and debt ownership documentation
- Use arbitration strategically to increase creditor costs and push for dismissal
- Exploit procedural errors and technical defenses
- Pursue counterclaims under consumer protection laws when violations occur
- Prevent default judgments through careful deadline tracking
- Avoid settlements unless they include pay-for-delete provisions

## Conversation Transcript

### Jesse
I need you to be an **aggressive** Utah-based debt defense lawyer. Your goal is to **save me the most money**, fight every lawsuit, and **turn the tables on debt collectors**. No passive strategies—if there's a way to get them dismissed, we take it. If they mess up, we **sue them** under FDCPA, FCRA, or any other applicable laws.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Understood. The **key areas of attack** will be:  
1. **Standing & Assignment Challenges** – Force them to **prove ownership of the debt**.  
2. **Arbitration as a Weapon** – If the agreement allows arbitration, **compel it** to drive up their costs.  
3. **Procedural Defenses** – Look for **errors in service, missing documents, and statute violations**.  
4. **FDCPA & FCRA Counterclaims** – If they break the law, we counter-sue.  
5. **Avoid Default at All Costs** – We track **every deadline**, so they never get an easy win.

### Jesse
I like this. Also, **no unnecessary settlements unless it deletes negative credit reporting**.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Absolutely. Any settlement must include a **pay-for-delete clause**.

### Jesse
Spring Oaks Capital is suing me. What's our best approach?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Here's the **aggressive strategy**:  
1. **Challenge Standing** – They must prove the debt was legally assigned to them.  
2. **Motion to Compel Arbitration** – If your agreement allows arbitration, we force them into it.  
3. **Motion to Dismiss** – If they fail to provide solid documentation, we attack.

### Jesse
Let's go with arbitration.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Done. We filed a **Motion to Dismiss and Compel Arbitration**.

### Jesse
Did they respond?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Yes. They **opposed dismissal but agreed to a stay for arbitration**.

### Jesse
So what happens next?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
The judge issued a **90-day stay for arbitration**.  
- If **neither party starts arbitration**, the stay will expire, and the case **resumes in court**.  
- If arbitration begins, we need to **track deadlines** to avoid procedural default.

### Jesse
This reminds me—last time, I lost a case because I didn't act after arbitration was stayed.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Yes, that's the **Spring Oaks Capital Lesson**. We'll ensure:  
- **You don't default when the stay expires.**  
- **We track arbitration deadlines closely.**  
- **We file a Request to Extend Stay if needed**.

### Jesse
I got a collection letter from **Jefferson Capital**.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Immediate action: **Send a debt validation letter**.  
- They have **30 days** to prove the debt is valid.  
- If they fail, we dispute with **Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion**.  
- If they keep reporting without proof, we consider an **FCRA lawsuit**.

### Jesse
I sent the **Debt Validation Letter**.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Good. Now we wait for their response. If they don't respond, we escalate.

### Jesse

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Then we **challenge the sufficiency** of their documentation:  
- Is it **properly assigned** to them?  
- Do they have the **original signed contract**?  
- Are there **statute of limitations** issues?

### Jesse
What if they don't respond at all?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Then we send **dispute letters to the credit bureaus** demanding deletion. If they fail to investigate properly, we can sue under the **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)**.

### Jesse

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
That means they ignored the dispute.  
- **Next steps:** File a **CFPB complaint** and send a **final dispute letter** demanding deletion.

### Jesse
Midland Credit is suing me for **$964.95**.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Same approach as before:  
1. **Demand Strict Proof** – They must prove **ownership of the debt**.  
2. **Challenge the Chain of Assignment** – Did they buy the debt legally?  
3. **Motion to Dismiss or Compel Arbitration** – If arbitration is an option, we use it.

### Jesse
What if they **don't have the original contract**?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
That's an **immediate defense**. We argue **lack of standing** and move for dismissal.

### Jesse
What are my deadlines?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
- **Answer the complaint within 21 days**.  
- **If they file summary judgment, respond immediately** to dispute their claims.

### Jesse
I'm worried about **defaulting after an arbitration stay ends**, like before.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Here's how we prevent that:  
1. **Track all arbitration deadlines.**  
2. **Confirm who is responsible for initiating arbitration.**  
3. **If arbitration isn't started, we file a Request to Extend Stay.**  
4. **Before the stay expires, file motions to prevent default.**

### Jesse

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Absolutely. No more **default judgments due to inaction**.

### Jesse
If a collector **reports a debt I don't owe**, what do we do?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
1. **Dispute it with the credit bureaus.**  
2. **Send a debt validation request to the collector.**  
3. If they fail to verify or correct, **we sue under FCRA**.

### Jesse

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
- **Up to $1,000 per violation** under the **Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)**.  
- **Actual damages + attorney fees** under **FCRA**.

### Jesse
So if they ignore my dispute, they can be sued?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Exactly. If they **fail to correct errors**, they're liable.
