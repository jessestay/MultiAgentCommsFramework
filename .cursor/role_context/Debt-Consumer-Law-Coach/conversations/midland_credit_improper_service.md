# Debt & Consumer Law Coach Key Conversation: Midland Credit Improper Service Defense

## Conversation Date: February 2025

## Context
This conversation addressed a strategic opportunity in the Midland Credit lawsuit where Jesse discovered he was allegedly served on January 31st, but was not actually at home during the claimed service time. The discussions focused on leveraging this improper service to potentially get the case dismissed, including procedural steps, evidence submission, and timing considerations.

## Key Points
- Identify improper service as a strong defense against debt collection lawsuit
- File Motion to Dismiss for Insufficient Service of Process before contacting opposing counsel
- Use doorbell camera footage as evidence that no service occurred
- Understand procedural requirements for submitting digital evidence to Utah courts
- Track response deadlines and Request to Submit for Decision timing
- Consider strategies for potential dismissal with prejudice

## Conversation Transcript

### Jesse
I just received a letter in the mail yesterday from a consumer debt law firm saying I was being sued by Midland Credit, offering to send an answer for me. This lawsuit was news to me, as I have not been served. I looked up the case on Utah's Xchange platform, and sure enough, a lawsuit has been filed. But the interesting part is that they say I was already served on January 31st, and that it was served to a white female, age 45-55 late evening that day. The problem is there was no one home at the time they say I was served—I was at orchestra practice during that time. I imagine I can also pull video from my doorbell camera from that time to prove no one came to the door.

So here's my question. Is it best for me to reach out to their lawyer and tell them I was not served so they can serve me again, or is it best I pretend I don't know about this lawsuit, let them file a judgment against me, and then get the case dismissed by filing for improper service? If I were properly served, I think seeking to claim the arbitration clause would be my best approach, but where I wasn't, I'm wondering if filing for improper service might be best. I might be too late to file an answer anyway.

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
The best course of action is to **immediately file a Motion to Dismiss for Insufficient Service of Process** before engaging with opposing counsel. If you notify them informally, they will correct their mistake and serve you properly, keeping the case alive. By filing the motion first, you force the court to decide on service validity **before** they can reattempt service. If granted, they would need to start over. Since your video footage shows no process server arrived, that serves as **critical evidence** to support dismissal.

### Jesse
If I file a motion to vacate the judgment under Rule 60(b), can they still serve me again?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Yes, but **only if the case is reopened** and they choose to restart the process. If your **Motion to Dismiss for Insufficient Service** is granted, the case is dismissed without prejudice, meaning they must refile and serve you properly from scratch. However, if the court denies your motion, they can attempt proper service without refiling. The key is ensuring the motion is strong enough to result in dismissal **before** they attempt to fix the issue.

### Jesse
What is the best way to get it dismissed with prejudice?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Typically, a dismissal **for insufficient service** is **without prejudice**, meaning the plaintiff can refile. However, dismissal **with prejudice** would prevent them from refiling. To strengthen your argument:
1. **Highlight willful misrepresentation** – If they knowingly filed a false affidavit of service, argue that they should not be given another chance.
2. **Argue undue prejudice** – If refiling would unfairly burden you (e.g., statute of limitations issues, improper delay), request dismissal with prejudice.
3. **Challenge standing** – If they cannot **prove they own the debt**, dismissal may be warranted on additional grounds.
4. **File an FDCPA counterclaim** – If their service attempt violated debt collection laws, it strengthens your leverage.

### Jesse
How do I attach the footage when it's video?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Utah courts typically require digital evidence to be submitted via **USB thumb drive** rather than online filing. The court clerk confirmed you must:
1. **Copy all video files onto a USB drive.**
2. **Prepare a cover letter listing the evidence** and explaining its relevance.
3. **Submit the USB drive in person or via certified mail.**
4. **Send a copy to Plaintiff's counsel as well.**
5. **File a Notice of Filing of Evidence via MyCase.**

### Jesse
Can they serve me again before the judge makes a judgment or responds to my motion to dismiss, where they already said they served me?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
**No**, they **cannot legally attempt service again** while your **Motion to Dismiss for Insufficient Service of Process** is still **pending before the judge.**

- The **court must rule on your motion first** before any new service attempt can be made.
- If they attempt service now, it is an **admission that their original service was defective**, which supports your motion.
- If the judge **grants your motion**, the case is dismissed, and they would have to **refile and start over**.
- If the judge **denies the motion**, then they can attempt to serve you properly, but **only after the ruling**.

If they attempt improper service before the judge rules, document it and be prepared to challenge it.

### Jesse
When should I file the Request to Submit for Decision? And should I avoid answering the door to the server if they try to serve me?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
You should file the **Request to Submit for Decision** if:
- The **plaintiff fails to respond within 14 business days** (March 11, 2025, in your case).
- If they do respond, wait until your **reply deadline expires (March 18, 2025)** and then file.
- Earliest RTS filing date (if no response) → **March 12, 2025**.
- Latest RTS filing date (if you file a reply) → **March 19, 2025**.

**Regarding service avoidance:**
- You are **not obligated to open the door or accept service**.
- If they leave documents without proper service, **it may still be invalid**.
- **If served correctly,** you will need to shift strategy to arbitration or another defense.
