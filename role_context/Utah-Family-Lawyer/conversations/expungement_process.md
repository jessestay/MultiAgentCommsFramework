# Utah Family Lawyer Key Conversation: Expungement Process

## Conversation Date: March 2025

## Context
This conversation addressed the process for expunging a dismissed criminal case from Jesse's record that involved charges related to Rebecca. The discussion covered eligibility requirements, procedural steps, automatic expungement limitations, and cost-saving options for completing the expungement process in Utah.

## Key Points
- Determine eligibility for expungement of dismissed charges
- Outline step-by-step process for obtaining expungement
- Explain why the case wasn't automatically expunged under Utah's Clean Slate Law
- Clarify misconceptions about pleas in abeyance and record clearance
- Identify free and low-cost options for completing the expungement
- Provide specific guidance for filing with South Jordan Justice Court

## Conversation Transcript

### Jesse
I have a criminal case with Rebecca where all charges were dismissed, but it still shows on my record. How can I remove this case from my record?

### Utah Family Lawyer
You'll need to go through the **Utah expungement process**. Since your charges were **dismissed with prejudice** and you completed a **plea in abeyance**, you should qualify for expungement. Here's what you need to do:

1. **Request your criminal history report** from Utah BCI ([bci.utah.gov](https://bci.utah.gov)).
2. **Apply for a Certificate of Eligibility** for expungement.
3. **File a Petition for Expungement** with the **South Jordan Justice Court**.
4. **Notify the prosecutor's office** (they have 35 days to object).
5. **Attend a hearing if required** and obtain the expungement order.

### Jesse
Why wasn't this automatically expunged?

### Utah Family Lawyer
Utah's **Clean Slate Law (HB 431)** allows automatic expungement for some cases, but **yours was not eligible** because:
- It involved **domestic violence-related charges**, which are excluded from automatic expungement.
- You **entered a plea in abeyance**, which requires **a separate expungement petition** under Utah law.
- Your case was in **South Jordan Justice Court**, and justice courts sometimes don't process automatic expungements properly.

### Jesse
Don't pleas in abeyance eventually expunge from the record?

### Utah Family Lawyer
No, **a plea in abeyance does not automatically expunge** in Utah. It allows the charge to be dismissed **after completing the agreement**, but the case **remains on record** until you **file for expungement**.

To remove it completely, you must:
1. **Get a Certificate of Eligibility** from Utah BCI.
2. **File an expungement petition** with the court.
3. **Serve notice to the prosecutor.**

Since your case was dismissed in **2017**, you are well past any waiting period and can **file for expungement immediately**.

### Jesse
Are there ways to get it expunged for free?

### Utah Family Lawyer
Yes! Here are some options:
1. **Fee Waiver:** Utah courts allow **low-income individuals** to apply for a **fee waiver** for court and BCI fees.
2. **Free Expungement Clinics:** Organizations like **Utah Legal Services** and **Clean Slate Utah** provide **free help** for eligible individuals.
3. **Pro Bono Attorneys:** The **Utah Pro Bono Project** and **law school legal clinics** may assist with expungements at no cost.
4. **Self-Filing:** If you don't want to pay attorney fees, I can **draft your expungement petition for free**, and you just need to file it yourself.
