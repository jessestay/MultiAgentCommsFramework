## Conversation Transcript

### Jesse
Can you provide an overview of the divorce process in Utah? What are the key steps and requirements someone should know?

### Utah Family Lawyer
Here's a comprehensive overview of the Utah divorce process:

1. **Residency Requirements**:
   - At least one spouse must have been a Utah resident for at least 3 months before filing
   - For cases involving children, the children generally must have lived in Utah for at least 6 months

2. **Filing Process**:
   - **Petition for Divorce**: Initial document filed with the court
   - **Financial Declaration**: Detailed disclosure of assets, debts, income, expenses
   - **Child Custody Information**: Parenting plan if children are involved
   - **Summons and Service**: Formal notification to the other spouse

3. **Mandatory Waiting Period**:
   - 30-day waiting period from filing to finalization
   - Can be waived in extraordinary circumstances
   - Actual timeline often longer depending on complexity

4. **Divorce Approaches**:
   - **Uncontested Divorce**: Both parties agree on all terms
   - **Contested Divorce**: Disagreement requiring court intervention
   - **Mediation**: Required in most cases before trial
   - **Collaborative Divorce**: Using collaborative law process

5. **Special Utah Requirements**:
   - Mandatory divorce education class for parents
   - Mandatory mediation in most cases
   - OCAP (Online Court Assistance Program) for self-represented parties

### Jesse
What are the most common issues that arise during Utah divorces, and how are they typically handled?

### Utah Family Lawyer
The most common issues in Utah divorces include:

1. **Property Division**:
   - Utah follows "equitable distribution" (fair but not necessarily equal)
   - Marital vs. separate property distinction
   - Business valuation often contested
   - Retirement accounts require Qualified Domestic Relations Orders (QDROs)

2. **Child Custody**:
   - Joint legal custody presumption in Utah
   - Physical custody based on best interests factors
   - Utah's minimum schedule guidelines
   - Right of first refusal provisions
   - Relocation restrictions (150+ miles requires court approval)

3. **Child Support**:
   - Calculated using Utah Child Support Guidelines
   - Based on income, number of children, and overnights
   - Medical insurance and childcare costs allocation
   - Modification requires substantial change in circumstances

4. **Alimony (Spousal Support)**:
   - Based on need, ability to pay, and length of marriage
   - Usually limited to length of marriage
   - Terminates on remarriage or cohabitation
   - Tax implications changed under recent tax law
