# Debt & Consumer Law Coach Key Conversation: Spring Oaks Case Appeal

## Conversation Date: March 2025

## Context
This conversation series addressed post-judgment strategy for the Spring Oaks Capital case, focusing on procedural options after a judgment was entered on July 26, 2023. The discussions covered potential motions to file, response deadlines, collection risks, and practical steps to protect Jesse's interests following an adverse judgment.

## Key Points
- Evaluate procedural options after judgment has been entered
- Assess risks and benefits of filing a Motion to Set Aside Judgment
- Identify collection risks and asset protection strategies
- Ensure proper notification of address changes to maintain communication
- Develop post-judgment strategy that minimizes further legal exposure

## Conversation Transcript

### Jesse
I filed a Motion to Dismiss for Lack of Standing. Now what deadlines should I track?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
The key deadline is Plaintiff's response, due **14 business days** from your filing date. If they oppose, you have **7 business days** to reply. If no opposition is filed, you should submit a **Request to Submit for Decision** to move the motion forward.

### Jesse
Do I need to withdraw the motion if I change my mind?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
No, you can let it stand, and the judge will either rule on it or deny it. Withdrawing could signal weakness to the Plaintiff.

### Jesse
The Plaintiff filed an opposition. What do you think?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Their primary argument is that judgment was already entered on July 26, 2023, so your Motion to Dismiss is invalid. They claim you failed to act within procedural deadlines, and the case is already resolved.

### Jesse
Does this mean I should file a Motion to Set Aside Judgment instead?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Possibly. If the judgment was entered **without considering arbitration**, or if you weren't given proper opportunity to challenge standing, you may have grounds under **Utah Rule 60(b)** to set it aside.

### Jesse
What are the risks of filing a Motion to Set Aside Judgment?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
1️⃣ **Judge may view it as a delay tactic** if no strong procedural error exists.  
2️⃣ **Plaintiff could respond aggressively**, filing additional affidavits and pursuing collection actions.  
3️⃣ **If denied, it reinforces the validity of the judgment**, making future challenges harder.

### Jesse
Is it worth it? What would you do?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Based on your case, **I do NOT recommend filing a Motion to Set Aside Judgment.** Instead, I recommend focusing on **negotiation and asset protection strategies**, as the risk of escalation outweighs the benefits.

### Jesse
What's the biggest collection risk now?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Since you **don't have wages to garnish** and **the Plaintiff doesn't know your bank details**, their biggest weapon is **a bank levy—if they find an account—or property seizure if exemptions don't cover your assets.**

### Jesse
Can they send the sheriff?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
It's unlikely unless they believe you own valuable assets beyond exemption limits. In Utah, certain personal property is **protected**, but we should check exemption laws to confirm your trumpet and truck are safe.

### Jesse
Do I need to notify the court of my new address?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Yes, you should file a **Notice of Change of Address** to ensure you receive all court communications. This prevents the Plaintiff from claiming you were properly served if they use an old address.

### Jesse
Can you draft it?

### Debt & Consumer Law Coach
Yes, I've prepared the Notice of Change of Address. You should file it with the court and send a copy to Plaintiff's attorney.

## Decisions Made
- Acknowledge that judgment was already entered on July 26, 2023
- Decide against filing a Motion to Set Aside Judgment due to risks outweighing benefits
- Focus on negotiation and asset protection strategies rather than further litigation
- File Notice of Change of Address to ensure receipt of court communications
- Verify Utah exemption laws to protect personal property from seizure
